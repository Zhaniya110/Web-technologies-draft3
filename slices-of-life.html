People plaing around with obesity problem , others sippig their big mugs with hot coffee, I study this people...

Journal, I decided to try the new orange punch at McDonalds. I was planning to write a descriptive paper on people I see for some time but was not sure if it a good idea. Although it is loud and too crowded, I found it helpful. I was trying to ignore the loudness and was more focused on getting the paper done as soon as possible. Here’s the result of me observing people at McDonalds. Observation of two young and totally ignorant girls who decided to indulge themselves in unhealthy diet names Fast Food.

Two girlfriends at McDonalds. There is this window wall separating them from other two female customers. The pretty one sits on a bench. She wears white knitted _ cardigan a navy t-shirt and jeans. Her dip dyed hair is sort to her shoulders and flat. She wears blur or green eyeshadows. The other girls ugly. Ugly friend or …sister? Anyway, so she’s in this horrible black hoodie. Big thick glasses and, oh my god, yellow spiral head scrunchy. I am sure that they’re sisters. Both has small jaws. Looks like they have no jaw at all. Funny people but hell they start to enrage me for some unknown reason. It is unfair for the ugly girl be a submissive, a saddler. She’s wearing black leather boots with side chain over burgundy tights.

I ordered an orange punch. It’s sweet but bitter. Bitter like a sweetened ginger tea. Well, I tried though. There are too many women. The fast-food restraint is popular among right bank city dwellers. Nevertheless, there are many women. Not equally distributed. My battery runs on fumes. I need to recharge my phone unless I get by without it. But I don’t have any cash or card.

Ok so these two seemingly young girls ordered 2 burgers 2 frees 2 coke paper mugs. The pretty hot one’s sipping on her coke. I don’t see her eat anything but these fries that don’t look delicious. They eat starter set of some nuggets and chicken in a white paper box with a plait yellow pattern. Small packets of fries in red. They sit from two sides. They sit on two small tables. From my experience I know how uncomfortable it is. There’s McCafé from their left side. I want a piece strawberry cheesecake. I don’t like this punch but out of sheer mechanism I drink it anyway.

For now, this is all I could make out of this couple. The hot beverage turned out to be a total swindle and waste of my $1.3. I wish, I ordered a cheesecake instead and a cup of tea or coffee. This was my fast-food restaurant visit, a good one. I wrote this essay.
